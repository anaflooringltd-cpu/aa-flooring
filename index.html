What we do

Four things, done properly.

We don't carpet, we don't tile. We focus on the surfaces we know inside-out, and we make the trim and stairs ourselves so the job comes out of one shop.

Hardwood Flooring

Solid & Engineered

We install both solid and engineered hardwood — species ranging from white oak and maple to acacia, teak, and walnut. Pre-finished or site-finished depending on what the project calls for.

Every install starts with a subfloor check. If the slab isn't flat or the moisture content is off, we sort that first. A floor is only as good as what's under it.

We also do herringbone and other pattern work for clients who want something that stands out. Adds time, not drama.

Vinyl & Laminate

Click-Lock & Glue-Down

LVP (luxury vinyl plank) is fully waterproof and handles the kind of abuse hardwood can't — pet claws, spills, basement moisture. We install both click-lock and glue-down formats depending on the subfloor situation.

Laminate is a smart choice for basement suites, rental units, and high-traffic areas where you want a wood look at a lower price point. We supply acoustic-rated underlay on every strata install.

Custom Accessories

Milled In-Shop

Transitions, reducers, T-moldings, quarter-round, and threshold pieces — milled in our Coquitlam shop to match whatever you're putting on the floor. Off-the-shelf trim almost never fits right.

If the floor crosses a height change or meets tile or carpet, we make an accessory that actually works. Matching species, matching stain, clean fit.

Stair Treads & Casings

Custom Fabricated

This is the work we're probably best known for. We build stair treads, risers, and casings to fit — curved stairs, open-sided stairs, waterfall edges, floating configurations. All fabricated in-shop from the same species as your floor.

A mismatched staircase undoes a beautiful floor. We don't let that happen.

What affects your final price

Subfloor condition

If the slab needs levelling, grinding, or moisture barrier work before installation, that adds cost. We check every subfloor before quoting — no surprises mid-job.

Room shape & layout

Straight rectangular rooms are fastest. Lots of door cuts, alcoves, or pattern work (herringbone) adds labour time. We'll call it out clearly in your quote.

Material grade & species

White oak and walnut carry a premium over maple or standard engineered. Wider boards waste more material. We can show you options at different price points during the site visit.

Demolition & disposal

Ripping out old carpet, laminate, or hardwood adds to the job. We handle demo and disposal — just included in the scope, not billed as a surprise at the end.

Access & building type

Strata buildings with elevator booking requirements or parking restrictions add a bit of logistics time. We're familiar with most Burnaby and Coquitlam strata buildings.

Stair complexity

Straight-run stairs are priced by the tread. Curved, open-sided, or floating staircases require more fabrication time and are quoted after an in-person measurement.

What a real job costs — examples

800 sqft of engineered hardwood in a Coquitlam townhouse: $9,600 – $12,800 fully installed, including subfloor prep on a flat slab. Add $1,000 – $2,500 if there's demo of existing carpet.

400 sqft of LVP in a Burnaby condo: $2,800 – $4,000 installed, including acoustic underlay (required by most strata bylaws). That's the most common job we do in strata buildings.

What makes the price go up

  • Subfloor levelling — if the slab is uneven, we fix it before anything goes down. Add $1 – $3/sqft depending on severity.
  • Demo of existing floors — carpet disposal runs $1 – $2/sqft; tile demo (which we refer out) runs higher.
  • Pattern work — herringbone adds roughly 15% to install time. Worth it for kitchens and entryways.
  • Premium species — walnut and wide-plank white oak cost more in material and waste more in cuts.
  • Strata access restrictions — elevator booking, move-in fees, and parking limits add a small logistics overhead.

Why we don't quote by phone

Any contractor who quotes you a firm price without seeing the subfloor is guessing — and usually guessing low to win the job, then adding surprises later. We do free in-person estimates because the condition of what's underneath the floor changes the number by 20–40% on older homes.

Estimates are free, take 30 minutes, and we come to you. We'll measure, check the subfloor, and hand you a quote before we leave your house.

Stairs April 2026

Custom stair treads in the Lower Mainland — what to know before you start

If your floor project stops at the staircase, the job isn't finished. Here's how custom stair treads work, what they cost, and why the stairs are actually the hardest part of any floor project.

Most flooring jobs end at the bottom step. The installer lays beautiful hardwood through the main floor, packs up, and leaves you with painted pine stairs that clash with everything. It's the most common complaint we hear from customers who used a different contractor.

Stairs are hard. They're the most visible part of any floor job, they take custom fabrication, and they require precise on-site fitting. Most flooring companies subcontract them or skip them entirely. We make them ourselves.

The three stair tread styles we build

Square nose (flush): The tread sits flush with the riser. Clean, modern, common in new builds. Easiest to fabricate, lowest cost.

Bullnose (rounded edge): The front edge of the tread has a routed profile that overhangs the riser slightly. Traditional look, common in character homes. Mid-range in cost.

Waterfall edge: The tread wraps over the riser edge in one continuous piece, creating a seamless "waterfall" of grain. This is the premium option — it's what you see in high-end renovations and it's what we're most known for. Requires more material and more precise fabrication.

What about curved staircases?

Curved stairs are where most contractors tap out. Each tread on a curved staircase is a unique shape — no two are identical. We measure every tread individually, fabricate each one in-shop, and do a dry-fit before final installation. It takes longer than a straight run, but the result is a staircase that looks like it was always there.

Materials — same species as your floor

The whole point of custom treads is that they match your floor. We fabricate from the same species, same stain, and ideally the same lot of material. Matching engineered hardwood, solid hardwood, or even LVP — we do all three. If you're doing a site-finish on the floors, we finish the treads at the same time so the colour is identical.

If we're ordering white oak for your main floor, adding stair treads from the same lot costs you nothing extra in coordination — and a lot less than trying to match it six months later.

Advice March 2026

LVP vs engineered hardwood for a Vancouver condo or basement

Moisture, strata bylaws, and budget — the three factors that decide what goes on your below-grade or condo floor. Here's the honest comparison.

This is the most common question we get from condo owners and people finishing basements in the Lower Mainland. The short answer: LVP wins below grade, engineered hardwood wins above grade when budget allows. Here's why.

The moisture problem in BC

Vancouver's climate is wet. Even above-grade condos have humidity swings that solid hardwood doesn't tolerate well — it expands and contracts, and in a concrete building with limited air circulation, that movement causes gapping and cupping over time. Below-grade (basements, basement suites, ground-floor concrete slabs) adds the risk of moisture coming up through the slab.

Solid hardwood is not appropriate for below-grade installations. Full stop. Even engineered hardwood is borderline on a damp slab. LVP is 100% waterproof and unaffected by moisture from below.

Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) — the practical choice

LVP has come a long way in the last five years. The current generation of 8mm+ thick click-lock planks looks and feels substantially different from the thin vinyl of ten years ago. The main advantages for BC homes:

  • Fully waterproof — safe below grade and in kitchens
  • Dimensionally stable — doesn't expand/contract with humidity
  • Comfortable underfoot with a good acoustic underlay
  • Pet and kid friendly — significantly more scratch-resistant than hardwood
  • Less expensive: $5 – $10/sqft installed vs $9 – $16/sqft for engineered hardwood

The downside: it's not real wood. You can't refinish it if it gets damaged — you replace it. And in a high-end renovation where the floors are a design statement, LVP reads as LVP to anyone paying attention.

Engineered hardwood — the premium choice above grade

Engineered hardwood has a real hardwood veneer (typically 2–4mm thick) bonded to a plywood core. It handles humidity better than solid hardwood while still looking and feeling like the real thing. It can be refinished once or twice over its lifetime, unlike LVP.

For main-floor living areas in condos above the second floor, engineered hardwood is usually the right call — especially if you're doing stair treads in the same material, which is where the look really pays off. Budget $9 – $16/sqft installed depending on species and plank width.

The strata acoustic rule you need to know

Most strata buildings in Burnaby, Coquitlam, and Vancouver require a minimum IIC (Impact Isolation Class) rating of 60 for hard flooring. This means you need an acoustic underlay between the slab and the floor — regardless of whether you're putting down LVP or engineered hardwood. We supply and include acoustic-rated underlay on every strata install. If you're doing your own research, ask your strata manager for the bylaw before you buy materials.

Our recommendation

Basement or below-grade slab: LVP, always. 8mm+ thick with built-in underlay or a separate acoustic pad. Don't put engineered hardwood below grade on a BC slab.

Above-grade condo, main living area: Engineered hardwood if budget allows, LVP if you want the lower price point or have pets/young kids. Both are appropriate — it's a lifestyle and budget call.

Rental suite: LVP. Easier to clean, more damage-tolerant, no refinishing required between tenants.
